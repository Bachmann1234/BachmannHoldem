# M4.5 Foundations primer — designer brief

**For:** a design pass (Claude Design / claude.ai/design, or a human designer) on the new
**"Learn the fundamentals"** path, _before_ any UI is built. This mirrors the M4 process: the M4 PWA
shell shipped from a design handoff bundle (see [`m4-pwa/`](m4-pwa/)), and we want the same for the
primer so the look-and-feel is decided up front, not improvised in code.

**What's already done (the pure logic is built and tested — do not redesign it):** the six lessons,
their copy, the retrieval checks, and the grading all exist in the `@holdem/curriculum` package. This
brief is a **visual + interaction** request only: design the screens that render this data, in our
existing design language. The engineering tickets ([`0046`](../../tickets/0046-pwa-learn-nav.md),
[`0047`](../../tickets/0047-pwa-lesson-player.md), [`0048`](../../tickets/0048-pwa-lesson-progress.md))
will wire your mockups to the package.

---

## 1. The product, in one paragraph

Bachmann Hold'em is a poker **trainer**. The play table has an on-demand coach that grades each
decision in numbers ("equity 25%, price 33% → leak"). That's correct, but it _assumes the player
already understands_ equity, pot odds, EV, position, and ranges. The **Foundations primer** teaches
those five-or-six ideas first — each as a **~30-second explanation followed by an interactive
question the player answers** (we teach by _retrieval_, because being tested makes it stick far
better than re-reading). It's a short on-ramp, **not a textbook**: six crisp lessons, then the player
is handed off to free play (and, later, drill sets).

## 2. Use the locked M4 design system — this is an extension, not a new app

The primer lives **inside the existing PWA** and must look of-a-piece with it. The source of truth is
[`m4-pwa/styles.css`](m4-pwa/styles.css) and [`m4-pwa/DESIGN-NOTES.md`](m4-pwa/DESIGN-NOTES.md).
Carry these forward exactly:

- **Direction: Playful** — warm glow, rounded (`--radius-panel: 24px`), accent-forward. Dark-first
  (`--bg #0d0f13`, surfaces `#161922`/`#1d212c`). Phone-first, **`max-width: 460px`** app column.
- **Accent: green** `--accent: oklch(0.82 0.16 158)` (`#3ddc84`), `--accent-ink #06120c`.
- **Verdict palette:** `--good` (green), `--bad` (red), `--warn` (amber) with their `-soft` fills.
- **Fonts:** Hanken Grotesk (UI/display), JetBrains Mono (numbers).
- **Tone: encouraging.** Warm, never scolding — "Close one, you'll catch it next time," not "WRONG."
- **Reuse existing components.** The graded-answer result should feel like the table's **coach
  drawer**: the same verdict **badge** (✓ / ! / ~), the **metric cards** (equity / pot-odds / EV),
  the **equity win/lose bar**, and the **explainer note**. A graded lesson check and a graded hand
  should look like siblings. (See `CoachDrawer.tsx` / `styles.css` `.verdict`, `.metric`, `.eq-bar`,
  `.coach-note`.)

## 3. Screens to design (three)

### 3a. Top-level navigation / home

**Problem:** the PWA today has **no navigation** — it boots straight into table setup. The primer
needs a way in that sits beside play. Design a top-level entry that offers at least **Play** and
**Learn the fundamentals** (room for a future **Drills** entry — M5). Decide the pattern (see open
questions). Keep it offline, instant, phone-first.

### 3b. The Learn lesson list / map

A screen listing the **six lessons in order** (the curriculum is sequential: each builds on the last).
For each lesson show its **title** and a sense of **what it teaches**, its **completion state** (done
/ in-progress / not-started), and let the player **resume at the next unfinished lesson**. Completing
all six surfaces a **hand-off** — a clear CTA toward free play (and a forward nod to drills). The six
lessons, in order, with their real titles:

1. **Equity:** your share of the pot
2. **Pot odds:** the price of a call
3. **The continue rule:** equity vs price
4. **EV:** counting the decision in chips
5. **Position:** acting later is an edge
6. **Ranges:** think in strength tiers

### 3c. The lesson player (the heart of it)

One lesson = a short teach, then one or more retrieval checks. The flow per lesson:

```
[explanation ~30s read]  →  [the check: a prompt + 2 answer buttons]
        →  player picks an answer  →  [graded result: ✓/✗ + the right answer + an explanation]
        →  advance to the next check / next lesson
```

Real example — the **continue-rule** lesson (this is exactly the data you'll render):

- **Explanation (the teach):** _"Here is the one rule that drives every call-or-fold decision:
  continue when your equity beats the price, fold when it does not. Equity is how good your hand is;
  the price is the pot odds the bet sets. Worth 40% and the price is 30%? Continue. Worth 20% and the
  price is 33%? Fold. Everything else postflop is detail on top of this single comparison."_
- **The check (prompt):** _"You hold A♠A♥ on A♣K♦7♥ — top set. Your opponent bets, bringing the pot
  to 100, and you must call just 10 — a price of only ~9%. Your hand is worth ~96%. Call or fold?"_
- **The answer choices:** two buttons — **Call** / **Fold**. (Every check is a small set of choices;
  the primer's are all binary call/fold or open/fold.)
- **The graded result** (returned by the engine after the player picks): whether they were
  **correct**, the **explanation** string (built from the real numbers, e.g. _"Nice — that's exactly
  right. Equity 96.x% vs pot-odds price 9.x%; calling is worth +N chips."_), and — for the
  postflop/coach-graded checks — the **equity / pot-odds / EV** numbers you can lay out in the
  familiar metric cards + equity bar. (Two lessons — position, ranges — are preflop/chart checks with
  **no equity/EV cards**; their result is the badge + a short rationale line, like the coach drawer's
  preflop mode.)

Design the **whole loop**: the reading state, the question state (cards rendered with the four-color
deck for the postflop spots — reuse the table's `Card`), the answer-selection affordance, and the
graded-result state, plus how the player moves forward. Show both an **all-correct** and a
**got-it-wrong** result, since the encouraging tone differs.

## 4. Constraints the design must respect

- **Offline / local-only.** No backend, no accounts. Progress is stored on-device. No network states.
- **Phone-first, installable PWA.** 460px column, safe-area insets, touch targets, the existing a11y
  bar (focus management, keyboard, dialog semantics — match the coach drawer).
- **Retrieval is the point.** Don't let the explanation balloon — it's the ~30s setup; the **question
  is the lesson.** Resist adding diagrams/walls of text. Depth lives in the feedback loop, not here.
- **The numbers are real and fixed.** Equity/price/EV come from the deterministic engine; design to
  display them, don't invent values. Percentages render via our shared formatters.
- **No new poker brain.** Same caveat as M4: any prototype "engine" is throwaway — we render the
  `@holdem/curriculum` + `@holdem/coach` output only.

## 5. Open design questions (please decide and note your rationale)

1. **Navigation pattern** — a home/menu screen you return to? a persistent bottom tab bar (Play /
   Learn / Drills)? a hamburger? Given it's phone-first and we'll add Drills later, lean toward
   something that scales to 3 destinations.
2. **Lesson list vs. learning "path/map"** — a plain list, or a more game-like vertical path with
   progress (Duolingo-ish)? Keep it light — six items.
3. **Coach-graded result layout** — reuse the full metric-cards + equity-bar treatment from the coach
   drawer, or a slimmer lesson-specific result? (They should still feel related.)
4. **Progress + resume affordance** — how completion and "resume here" read on the list.
5. **End-of-primer hand-off** — what the completion celebration + CTA-to-play looks like.

## 6. Deliverables (same shape as the M4 bundle)

Drop the artefacts under `docs/design/m4.5-foundations/` so the build can recreate them faithfully:

- **Annotated mockups** of the three screens (and the lesson-player states: read / ask / graded-right
  / graded-wrong / end-of-primer).
- **CSS** for any new components, in the existing token system (extend `styles.css`; don't fork the
  palette/fonts). New classes should slot beside the existing `.verdict` / `.metric` / `.drawer`
  family.
- A short **design-notes** file capturing the locked decisions (your answers to §5) and a
  **design→build component map** like M4's, so the engineering tickets know what maps to what.
- Optionally a `design-chat` transcript, as M4 kept, since "the intent lives there."
