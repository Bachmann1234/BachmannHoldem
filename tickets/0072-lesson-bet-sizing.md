## Context

The pot-odds lesson teaches a beginner how to _respond_ to bet sizes (a third-pot is ~20%, half-pot
~25%, full-pot ~33%) but never how to _choose_ one when they are the bettor. A learner finishes the
primer able to face a bet but not to make one intelligently — yet the play loop and the M5.5 drills
constantly ask them to size bets (beginner-pedagogy review, 2026-06-16). This is the bettor-side
counterpart to the pot-odds lesson.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] A new `FOUNDATIONS` lesson on choosing a bet size: value vs. protection vs. bluff intent, and
      how size relates to the pot-odds price it lays the opponent (the inverse of what the pot-odds
      lesson taught), tagged with its `Concept` ([[0043-coach-concept-tag]]).
- [ ] ~30-second beginner-pitched explanation that connects "the size I pick is the price I'm
      offering" back to the pot-odds pegs the learner already knows.
- [ ] A retrieval check. **Gradability is the open question:** the coach grades whether to
      _continue_, not what size to _bet_. Resolve one of: - lean on the minimal, clearly-flagged declarative-check escape hatch from
      [[0045-foundations-primer-content]] (documented in the lesson, never contradicting the coach), or - motivate a small coach/curriculum addition that can rule on a sizing choice.
      Pick the lightest option that stays coach-true; do not author an answer key the live coach
      could contradict.
- [ ] Test + purity per the [[0045-foundations-primer-content]] bar.
